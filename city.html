Heart, the center of art and miniature painting in Afghanistan.

Painting Art in Herat Herat is one of the most important centers of painting and art in the history of Afghanistan and the Islamic world. During the Timurid period (15th century), Herat became a major cultural capital where the famous Herat School of Miniature Painting was formed. Characteristics of Herat Painting Delicate and detailed miniature paintings Use of bright and rich colors such as blue, gold, green, and red Great attention to details in faces, clothing, and nature Harmony between text and image, especially in illustrated manuscripts Depiction of daily life, literary stories, historical and religious scenes Famous Artist Kamal al-Din Behzad, the most renowned painter of the Herat School His works are considered masterpieces of Islamic miniature art and continue to inspire artists today. Subjects of the Paintings Stories from the Shahnameh Classical Persian literature by poets such as Rumi, Saadi, and Nezami Court life, social scenes, and spiritual themes Herat Today Even today, Herat remains an important center for traditional arts, with active painters, art schools, and cultural institutions.
